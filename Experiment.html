The Toyota AE86, also known as the Corolla Levin or Sprinter Trueno, is a compact, lightweight, rear-wheel-drive sports coupe known for its handling and performance. It's powered by a 1.6-liter inline-4 engine (4A-GE) producing around 120-130 horsepower depending on the market and emissions regulations. The AE86 is famed for its involvement in drifting and has a strong following among enthusiasts. Key Specifications: Engine: 1.6-liter 4A-GE inline-4, DOHC 16-valve, with T-VIS. Horsepower: 112-130 bhp (84-96 kW) depending on the market. Torque: 97-107 lb-ft (132-145 Nm). Weight: Approximately 2,200-2,300 lbs (998-1,043 kg). Drivetrain: Rear-wheel drive, 5-speed manual (some markets offered automatic). Suspension: MacPherson strut front, four-link live axle rear. Brakes: Disc brakes front and rear (some lower-spec models had rear drum brakes). Top Speed: Around 120 mph (193 km/h). Other notable features: Lightweight: The AE86 was designed to be lightweight, enhancing its agility and handling. Balance: The car is known for its balanced chassis and neutral handling characteristics. Availability: The AE86 was produced from 1983 to 1987 and is considered a classic JDM car.
